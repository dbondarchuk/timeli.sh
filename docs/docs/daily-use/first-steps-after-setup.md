# First steps in a new workspace

Once first-time setup sends you to the **dashboard**, use this short checklist so your booking page matches how you really work.

## Get comfortable with the layout

- **Dashboard**  -  today’s snapshot: upcoming visits, quick links, anything Timelish wants you to notice first.
- **Appointments**  -  every booking, filters, and tools to add or move items by hand.
- **Services**  -  the bookable offers clients see; add fields, add-ons, or discounts here as you grow.
- **Website**  -  pages, images, headers, and footers that face the public.
- **Settings**  -  business name, brand colours, booking rules, messages, and more (see [Change settings](./settings)).

![Dashboard home](/img/placeholders/daily-dashboard-home.svg)

## Double-check your public booking address

Open **Settings**  -  **Brand** and read the line that shows your **booking website address**. Copy it with the copy button and paste it into a private browser tab - what you see is close to what clients see.

If something looks unfinished, fix **pages** or **brand** settings before you share the link widely.

![Copy your booking link](/img/placeholders/daily-booking-link.svg)

## Refine services and hours

Visit **Services**  -  **Options** (or the area where your offers live) to adjust names, prices, and lengths. Then open **Schedule** from the **Appointments** area to align default working hours with reality.

![Services list](/img/placeholders/daily-services-list.svg)

## Connect what you still skipped

If you deferred calendars, meetings, or payments during setup, open **Apps**  -  **App Store** and finish those links now so reminders and checkouts behave the first week.

## Send yourself a test booking

From your public page, book a time as if you were a client. Cancel or move it afterward. This proves notifications, calendar blocks, and payment steps (if any) feel right.
