# Publish and share your booking link

A finished page is not live until you **publish** it and **save**. Once your key pages are live, copy your booking link and share it where clients will find you.

## How publishing works

Timelish does not use a separate "Publish" button. Each page goes live when you:

1. Open the page in **Website** → **Pages**.
2. Open the page **settings** panel (often a gear or settings icon while editing).
3. Turn on **Published**.
4. Set a **publish date** - use today or earlier so the page is visible now.
5. Tap **Save**.

Repeat for every page clients should see. Unpublished pages stay hidden from the public site.

Check the pages table: a published page shows that status in the list. Your home page and booking-related pages should be published before you share your link widely.

## Find your booking link

1. Open **Settings** → **Brand**.
2. On the **Brand** tab, find your **booking website address** - for example `https://yourname.timeli.sh`.
3. Use the **copy** button and paste the link somewhere safe.

Open the link in a **private or incognito** browser window. What you see is close to what clients see (without you being logged in as the owner).

## Preview vs live

- **Preview** (while editing) shows draft content with a special preview link - useful before you publish.
- **Live site** shows only **published** pages whose publish date has passed.

If something looks unfinished on the live link, return to **Website** → **Pages** or **Settings** → **Brand** to fix content or styling.

## Use your own web address (optional)

You can keep the default `yourname.timeli.sh` address or point a domain you own - like `www.yourbusiness.com` - at your Timelish site. That is optional for launch day.

When you are ready, follow **[Connect your own web address](/docs/daily-use/connect-domain)**.

## Share your link

Once you are happy with the live site, put your booking link where clients already look for you:

- Instagram, Facebook, or LinkedIn bio and posts
- Google Business Profile or other directory listings
- Email signature
- Printed cards or flyers
- A "Book now" button on an existing website

You can update the link later if you add a custom domain - just refresh those same places.
